more about peroskite

perovskite is a yellow, brown and grayish black mineral consisting of an oxide of calcium, titanium A and sometimes containing rare earth elements, or a perovskite can also be any materials with a crystal structure similar to the mineral called perovskite, which consist of titanium oxide (CaTiO3), The mineral was first discovered in the Ural mountains of Russia by Gustav Rose in 1839 and named after Russian Mineralogist L . A Perovski (1792-1856), Helen Dick Mc Gas, published X-ray diffraction patterns of barium titanate that reveal its perovskite structure, The ideal perovskite structure is cubic (Fig. 12A), but natural perovskite shows a displacement of both Ca and O relative to Ti that reduces the symmetry to orthorthombic. This structure can be viewed as a network of titled Ti-O octahedral with the Ca situated between these octahedral (Fig. 12B). the degree of tilt or twist of the octahedral allows many other elements to be accommodated within this structure in both natural and synthetic perovskites. The tilting has been described by angles a, b, y, to each of three orthogonal crystallographic axes, and the polarity with respect to the origin. CaTiO3 is accommodated by tilts in all three directions, with the tilt parallel to two axes having the same magnitude and polarity, whilst the tilt along the third axis differs in both magnitude and polarity.

PEROVSKITE MATERIALS

Materials with the same types of crystal structure as that of CaTiO3 were know as perovskite materials. The general formula used to describe perovskite materials is ABX3, where A and B are cations with A larger than that of B and X is the anion usually halogens. These structural typically have a cubic or tetragonal cystal structure with the stoichoiometry of AMX3, wehre both A and M are metal cations and X is an anion (Fig. 3.1) Each cation M is octahedrally coordinated with the anions X to form the basic building block of the perovskite structure. These MX6 octahedra are connected in a three-dimensional corner-sharing configuration, with the cation a embedded in the space formed between adjacent MX6 octahedron to neutralize the charge of the structure. TYPES OF PEROVSKITE

Perovskite materials are classified based on the type of anion (O2− or X−). There is the chalcogenide perovskite (ABO3) and the halide perovskite (ABX3). The halide perovskites are further classified as alkali halide and organometallic halide (Verma et al., 2018)

1.2.1 Chalcogenide Perovskite

Chalcogenide perovskite can also be classified by cation valence, A+B5+O3 (1, 5); A2+B4+O3 (2, 4) and A3+B3+O3 (3, 3) and these alone covers a large range of compounds. Many interesting electrical and magnetic properties can be found in the chalcogenide perovskite and these depend chiefly on the character of the B cation. For example, the d electrons of the transition metals at the B site have a marked effect creating a wide range of electrical behavior. Most chalcogenide compound are semi-conductors, however, a few such as SrVO3 and SrRuO3 show metallic conductivity and materials such as LaAlO3 and CaTiO3 have been shown to have ionic conductivity (Kilner et al., 1991). Similarly, the range of magnetic properties are large; for example, LaMnO3 is ferromagnetic whereas SrVO3 displays Pauli Paramagnetism (Kilner et al., 1991). Some examples of chalcogenide halides include strontium titanate, calcium titanate, lead titanate, bismuth ferrite, lanthanum ytterbium oxide etc. (Kilner et al., 1991). 1.2.2 Organometallic Halide Perovskite

These are often referred to as organic–inorganic perovskites as it is hybrid. The organic cation A usually methylammonium (CH3NH3+), ethylammonium (CH3CH2NH3+) and formamidinium (NH2CHNH2+) are used. The cation B is commonly Pb and sometimes Sn is used because it forms similar compounds with more ideal bandgap but exhibits lower stability due to the oxidation of Sn2+ to Sn4+ in the iodide perovskite) (Aslam, et al., 2021) Organometallic halide has a good opt electric properties for solar applications, for instance it is characterized by a high dielectric constant a direct bandgap near the optimum value for the highest conversion efficiency, a high absorption coefficient, high mobility and small effective masses with the electron and hole mobility in the same range enchanting the bipolar conduction. The best organometallic halide is CH3NH3Pb13. The inclusion of toxic Pb is also undesirable. There are research efforts to substitute Pb or to mitigate it toxicity by tight encapsulation of the solar cell and end of life recycling (Zekry et al., 2018)

Abstract

Perovskite solar cell (PSC) discovered by Lev Perovski in the year 2009 as a new class in the third generation photovoltaic (PV) technology, evolved from dye sentisizer solar cells (DSSCs), that holds a major potential for future renewable energy The rapid improvement of perovskite solar cells has made them the rising star of the photovoltaics world and of huge interest to the academic community. Since their operational methods are still relatively new, there is great opportunity for further research into the basic physics and chemistry around perovskites. Furthermore, as has been shown over the past few years - the engineering improvements of perovskite formulations and fabrication routines has led to significant increases in power conversion efficiency, with recent devices reaching over 23%, as of June 2018.
